Early History

Before the rise of the Russian Empire, the region where the Tunguska Nature Reserve resides use to be the home of the Evenki people. The Evenki people are mixed between Mongolia, China, and Russia and are believed to have inhabited this region for several thousand years, being made up of various clans that all invoked a common ancestor spirit in their prayers (Britannica, 2019).

The Evenki people would rely on foraging in the boreal forest and would use reindeer as multipurpose livestock. Trade was facilitated by fur and agriculture (Ermolova, 2017).

As the Russian Revolution of 1917 came to an end the Evenki in Siberia were organized into collective farms and were later organized in an autonomous district. After the fall of the Soviet Union the Evenki lands were assimilated into the Russian Siberian Federal District (Britannica, 2019).

Today, the Evenki People are not allowed to enter the Tunguska Nature Reserve. The reserve falls under the Russian policy of ‘Zapovednik’ and cannot be entered unless you have special clearance for scientific studies or other means.
