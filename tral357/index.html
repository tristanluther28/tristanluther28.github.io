Welcome & General Information!

Hello and welcome to this informational website on the Tunguska Nature Reserve informational webpage. Disclaimer: This website is in no way affiliated with the Tunguska Nature Reserve operations, management as well as the Russian Ministry on Natural Resources. This is a project for TRAL 357: Protected Areas and Protected Areas Management at Oregon State University.

The Tunguska Nature Reserve is in the Evenki District of the Krasnoyarsk Territory in Russia (See the map below). Established in 1995, the park was established due to its link with the 1908 Tunguska Event in which a powerful explosion occurred in the forest. The explosion is believed to be the result of a massive meteorite (Russian Ministry of Natural Resources, 2011). Making the primary reason for establishing the park to study the effect of an extraterrestrial collision on an ecosystem. The protected area is not open to the public, being reserved for scientific studies and experiments (Russian Ministry of Natural Resources, 2019). This would classify this region as Category 1b, a wilderness area according to the IUCN (IUCN, 2019). See the general facts chart for some more information.

The primary objectives for the scientists and employees that are permanently stationed at the Tunguska Reserve are to organization and conduct of scientific research, implementation of environmental monitoring, environmental education of the population, and participation in the state environmental review of projects and layouts of economic and other facilities (Russian Ministry of Natural Resources, 2011). The tasks outlined by the management mostly follow a preservationist philosophy when managing this protected area. Again, many of the studies are to see how a meteorite has affected the natural ecosystem so influence from certain conservationist practices would alter the current experiments being conducted at the reserve.
